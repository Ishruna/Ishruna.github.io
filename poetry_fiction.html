Phoenix of Rust and Gold


May 23, 2021
Dedicated to our grandmother who left us on May 23, 2021. I wrote this poem in an attempt to enshrine her essense.

Your Hair, the colour of autumn leaves
Smile, the first monsoon rain
Embrace, a summer veil, tender and tranquil.

Roots, twisted and twined in your veins
Stories in your wrinkles, riveting and mundane.
Blossoms spreading beyond neverland,
Your pensive eyes, true and unforgotten.

Go on, you child of green
Follow the path of the first and serene
Smiles and tears and void and still
We bid you farewell.

Rest in peace, you beautiful rose,
A phoenix of rust and gold,
Rest in peace, Mini.

My Troubled Muse


January 1, 2023

Tears at you, ripping you apart,
the push and pull of gray tides,
a sickening nightmare.

You struggle for the shore,
Unyielding, yet fragile.
Your legs go numb,
gasping for one last breath.

The waves long to engulf you.
At last, you succumb.
Is it so awful?
Perhaps, it is not.

For I am here, my dearest
Floating beside you, my ghost
Scorched and perished, a thousand times,
crossing over the burning bridge.

I offer you my last flame,
though flickering and dying,
to keep you afloat.
Because your life is my delight.

Breathe, my muse.
Live one more day.
In the late hours of the night,
n the hidden valleys and shadows,
may you find me.
